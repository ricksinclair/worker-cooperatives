# How to Start a Worker Cooperative in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania offers one of the most supportive legal frameworks for worker cooperatives in the United States. This guide walks you through the essential steps to form and operate a worker cooperative in the Commonwealth.

---

## 1. Quick Summary: What Makes Pennsylvania Unique

Pennsylvania stands out as one of the easier states to form a worker cooperative because the Commonwealth has a dedicated statutory framework specifically for this business type. Under [Title 15, Chapter 77](https://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/LI/CT/HTM/15/00.071..HTM) of the Pennsylvania Consolidated Statutes, workers' cooperative corporations are recognized as a distinct legal entity separate from other business forms like traditional corporations and partnerships.

**Key advantages of forming a worker cooperative in Pennsylvania:**

- **Dedicated statutory framework**: Chapter 77 specifically governs workers' cooperative corporations, providing clear legal recognition
- **Strong regional ecosystems**: Philadelphia has emerged as a major hub for cooperative enterprise
- **Multiple technical assistance providers**: Organizations like PACA provide hands-on startup support
- **Growing employee ownership movement**: The Pennsylvania Center for Employee Ownership actively promotes worker ownership statewide

### The Philadelphia Worker Cooperative Scene

Philadelphia has emerged as a hub for cooperative enterprise in Pennsylvania. The city is home to a vibrant ecosystem of worker cooperatives spanning multiple industries:

- **[Home Care Associates](https://philadelphia.coop/phillycoops/)** - A worker-owned home care service provider offering in-home medical care, personal care, and household services with an intensive skill-building training program
- **[Childspace Management Group](https://philadelphia.coop/phillycoops/)** - A worker cooperative whose member-owners are staff of Childspace Day Care Centers, dedicated to creating quality day care jobs
- **[Hivemind](https://philadelphia.coop/phillycoops/)** - A cooperatively-owned construction and fabrication company in West Philadelphia comprised of artists and craftspeople
- **[A&Associates](https://philadelphia.coop/phillycoops/)** - A worker-owned bookkeeping cooperative believing cooperative business is essential to building a better world

The [Philadelphia Area Cooperative Alliance (PACA)](https://philadelphia.coop/) serves as the regional anchor organization - a "co-op of co-ops" representing worker co-ops, food co-ops, housing co-ops, credit unions, energy co-ops, and school co-ops across the Philadelphia region. PACA members collectively represent over 20,000 member-owners within their cooperative network.

As of recent data, Pennsylvania hosts approximately 809 cooperatives with 3.6 million members, generating wages of $488 million for approximately 14,500 employees.

## 2. Best-Fit Legal Structures for Worker Cooperatives in Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania offers two primary legal pathways for forming a worker cooperative. Each has distinct advantages depending on your cooperative's size, goals, and complexity.

### Option A: Workers' Cooperative Corporation (15 Pa.C.S. Chapter 77)

This is Pennsylvania's dedicated statutory framework for worker cooperatives. Under [15 Pa.C.S. ยง 7703](https://law.justia.com/codes/pennsylvania/2014/title-15/chapter-77), Pennsylvania permits the formation of a workers' cooperative by "five or more farmers, mechanics, laborers or other persons who have incorporated themselves together" for the purpose of carrying on agricultural or commercial business.

**Key Features:**
- Specifically designed for worker ownership
- One member, one vote governance structure (regardless of shares held)
- Corporate name must contain "cooperative" or an abbreviation
- Subject to Chapters 1, 5, and 77 of Title 15
- Patronage rebates and distributions to members are permitted
- Clear governance structure with required board of directors

**Relevant Statutes to Review:**
- [Chapter 71](https://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/LI/CT/HTM/15/00.071..HTM) - Cooperative Corporations Generally
- [Chapter 77](https://law.justia.com/codes/pennsylvania/2014/title-15/chapter-77) - Workers' Cooperative Corporations
- [Chapter 5](https://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/LI/CT/HTM/15/15.HTM) - Corporations (general provisions)
- Chapter 1 - General Provisions

**Best For:** Cooperatives with five or more worker-owners seeking a legally recognized cooperative structure with clear democratic governance requirements built into the statute.

### Option B: Cooperative LLC

A Limited Liability Company (LLC) can be structured as a worker cooperative through a carefully drafted operating agreement that establishes democratic governance and worker ownership principles.

**Key Features:**
- Flexible management structure (can be member-managed or manager-managed)
- Operating agreement governs internal relations under [15 Pa.C.S. ยง 8815](https://www.palegis.us/statutes/consolidated/view-statute?iFrame=true&txtType=HTM&ttl=15&div=0&chpt=88&sctn=15&subsctn=0) (amended 2024)
- No minimum member requirement
- Pass-through taxation by default (can elect corporate treatment)
- Operating agreement is an internal document (not filed with the state)

**Template Resources:**
- [Democracy at Work Institute (DAWI)](https://becomingemployeeowned.org/learn/structure/llc/) provides template operating agreements for worker cooperative LLCs
- [ICA Group](https://institute.coop/news/guide-worker-cooperative-bylaws-and-operating-agreements) offers guidance on bylaws and operating agreements for democratic firms

**Best For:** Smaller cooperatives, startups with fewer than five members, or groups seeking maximum flexibility in governance design.

### Comparison Table

| Feature | Workers' Cooperative Corporation | Cooperative LLC |
|---------|----------------------------------|-----------------|
| Minimum Members | 5 | 1 (practically 2+) |
| Statutory Recognition | Explicit (Chapter 77) | Through operating agreement |
| Naming Requirement | Must include "cooperative" | Flexible |
| Default Taxation | Corporate | Pass-through |
| Governance Documentation | Articles + Bylaws | Certificate + Operating Agreement |
| Filing Fee | $125 | $125 |
| Cooperative Principles | Statutory protection | Contractual protection |
| Long-term Stability | Built-in protections | Depends on drafting quality |

Given the complexity of cooperative law, consulting with an attorney experienced in cooperative formation is recommended to determine the best structure for your specific situation.

## 3. Step-by-Step Formation Checklist

### Step 1: Business Name Search and Reservation

**Search for Name Availability:**
- Use the [Pennsylvania Department of State Business Search](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dos/programs/business) to check if your desired name is available
- For cooperative corporations, your name must contain "cooperative" or an abbreviation (e.g., "Co-op")
- For LLCs, name must include "LLC," "L.L.C.," or "Limited Liability Company"

**Reserve Your Name (Optional):**
- File a Name Reservation with the Bureau of Corporations and Charitable Organizations
- Reservation holds the name for 120 days
- Fee: $70

### Step 2: Appoint a Registered Office

Pennsylvania requires a registered office (not a registered agent in the traditional sense) for service of process under [15 Pa.C.S. ยง109](https://www.harborcompliance.com/pennsylvania-registered-agent).

**Requirements:**
- Must be a physical address in Pennsylvania (no P.O. boxes)
- Must be able to accept legal documents during business hours
- Can be your business address or a [Commercial Registered Office Provider (CROP)](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dos/programs/business/information-services/commercial-registered-office-providers)
- If using an individual, they must be a Pennsylvania resident

**Change of Registered Office:**
- Filing fee: $5 for domestic entities, $250 for foreign entities
- File online or by mail with the Department of State

### Step 3: File Formation Documents

**For Workers' Cooperative Corporation:**

1. Prepare Articles of Incorporation using the appropriate Department of State form
2. Complete the cooperative-specific sections required for cooperative corporations
3. Include a Docketing Statement with your filing
4. Articles must establish:
   - Qualifications for membership
   - Common bond among members by reason of occupation, residence, or otherwise
   - Statement that it is a cooperative corporation

**For Cooperative LLC:**

1. Prepare Certificate of Organization
2. Draft a comprehensive Operating Agreement establishing democratic governance
3. Include Docketing Statement

**Filing Options:**
- **Online:** [file.dos.pa.gov](https://file.dos.pa.gov) (strongly recommended - automatic review and approval)
- **Mail:** Pennsylvania Department of State, Bureau of Corporations and Charitable Organizations, P.O. Box 8722, Harrisburg, PA 17105-8722
- **In Person:** Available at bureau offices

**Filing Fee:** $125

**Publication Requirement:** Pennsylvania requires publication in two newspapers (one must be a legal newspaper or publication). No specific timeline mandated, but completion is recommended promptly.

### Step 4: Obtain Federal Employer Identification Number (EIN)

- Apply online at [IRS.gov](https://www.irs.gov/businesses/small-businesses-self-employed/apply-for-an-employer-identification-number-ein-online) for immediate issuance
- Required for tax filing, opening bank accounts, and hiring employees
- No fee

### Step 5: Register with Pennsylvania Department of Revenue

Register for state taxes using the [Pennsylvania Online Business Tax Registration](https://www.pa.gov/services/revenue/register-my-business-for-taxes) at [mypath.pa.gov](https://mypath.pa.gov).

**Common Tax Accounts:**
- **Employer Withholding Tax:** Required if you have employees; Pennsylvania withholds at a flat rate of 3.07%
- **Sales and Use Tax:** If selling taxable goods or services
- **Corporate Net Income Tax:** For cooperative corporations
- **Business Use Tax:** For items purchased without sales tax

**Important Notes:**
- You will receive separate account numbers for each tax type
- Account details arrive via email and mail within 7-10 business days
- Pennsylvania has reciprocal tax agreements with Indiana, Maryland, Ohio, New Jersey, Virginia, and West Virginia

**Contact:** Pennsylvania Department of Revenue at (717) 787-1064

### Step 6: Register with Department of Labor and Industry

If you have employees, register for:
- **Unemployment Compensation (UC):** Through the [UC Management System](https://www.uc.pa.gov/)
- **Workers' Compensation Insurance:** Required coverage for employees

### Step 7: Obtain Local Licenses and Permits

**Philadelphia Requirements:**

1. **[Commercial Activity License (CAL)](https://www.phila.gov/services/permits-violations-licenses/get-a-license/business-licenses/):**
   - Required for all businesses operating in Philadelphia (including those located outside city limits doing business in the city)
   - Free of charge
   - Does not require renewal
   - Apply through the [Department of Licenses and Inspections](https://www.phila.gov/departments/department-of-licenses-and-inspections/)
   - Apply online via [eCLIPSE](https://eclipse.phila.gov/) or in person at Permit and License Center (1401 JFK Blvd.)

2. **[Zoning Permit](https://www.phila.gov/services/permits-violations-licenses/apply-for-a-permit/zoning-permits/get-a-zoning-permit/):**
   - Required for your specific use of building/property
   - Apply in person at the Permit and License Center or online via eCLIPSE
   - Processing: 15-20 business days (5 days expedited for $1,050)

**Other Pennsylvania Municipalities:**
- Contact your local township, borough, or city for specific requirements
- Use the [PA Business One-Stop Shop](https://business.pa.gov/register/local-registrations-permits-zoning/) for guidance

### Step 8: File Annual Reports

Beginning in 2025, Pennsylvania requires [Annual Reports](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dos/programs/business/types-of-filings-and-registrations/annual-reports) for most business entities:

| Entity Type | Due Date | Fee |
|-------------|----------|-----|
| Corporations | June 30 | $7 |
| LLCs | September 30 | $7 |
| Nonprofits | June 30 | $0 |
| LPs/LLPs | December 31 | $7 |

**Important:**
- File online at [file.dos.pa.gov](https://file.dos.pa.gov) (strongly recommended)
- **Transition period (2025-2026):** No dissolution for failure to file
- **Beginning 2027:** Failure to file may result in administrative dissolution six months after due date
- The previous decennial report requirement has been repealed

## 4. Governance and Documents You Should Prepare

### Required Formation Documents

**For Cooperative Corporations:**
- Articles of Incorporation (filed with state)
- Bylaws (internal document)
- Docketing Statement (filed with state)

**For Cooperative LLCs:**
- Certificate of Organization (filed with state)
- Operating Agreement (internal document)

### Essential Internal Governance Documents

**1. Bylaws (Cooperative Corporation) or Operating Agreement (LLC)**

Should address:
- Membership requirements and process for becoming a member-owner
- Capital contributions (member buy-in amounts)
- Voting rights and procedures (one member, one vote)
- Board of directors structure and election process
- Officer roles and responsibilities
- Meeting requirements (annual, special, notice periods)
- Patronage allocation and distribution policies
- Member separation procedures (resignation, termination, death)
- Dispute resolution mechanisms
- Amendment procedures
- Protections against removing cooperative provisions (especially for LLCs)

**2. Membership Agreement**
- Individual contract between the cooperative and each member-owner
- Defines rights, responsibilities, and expectations
- Capital contribution schedule
- Probationary period terms (typically 6-12 months)

**3. Personnel Policies/Employee Handbook**
- Compensation structure
- Work schedules and time-off policies
- Performance evaluation procedures
- Grievance procedures
- Health and safety policies

**4. Financial Policies**
- Fiscal year designation
- Banking and signature authority
- Budget approval process
- Patronage dividend allocation formula
- Internal capital account procedures
- Reserve fund requirements

**5. Decision-Making Protocols**
- Defines which decisions require full membership vote vs. management authority
- Establishes consensus vs. majority voting thresholds
- Committee structures and authorities
- Conflict resolution processes

### Template Resources

- [Democracy at Work Institute](https://becomingemployeeowned.org/) - Template operating agreements and bylaws

- [U.S. Federation of Worker Cooperatives](https://www.usworker.coop/benefits/) - Member resources and templates

## 6. Worker-Ownership Conversions

Converting an existing business to worker ownership is an increasingly popular succession strategy in Pennsylvania, particularly as baby boomer business owners approach retirement.

### Why Consider Conversion?

- Preserves jobs and keeps businesses local
- Rewards employees who contributed to company success
- Maintains company culture and mission
- Potential tax benefits for selling owners
- Builds community wealth

### Conversion Pathways

**Direct Conversion to Worker Cooperative**
- Current owner sells business directly to employees organized as a cooperative
- Often financed through combination of seller notes, member equity, and cooperative lenders
- Best for smaller businesses (typically under 20 employees)
- May be more feasible for smallest businesses compared to ESOPs

**Hybrid ESOP-Cooperative Conversion**
- Some businesses use ESOP for initial acquisition, then convert to full cooperative
- Takes advantage of ESOP tax benefits for selling owner (1042 rollover)
- May work for businesses seeking gradual transition

### Resources for Conversions

**[Pennsylvania Center for Employee Ownership](https://ownershippennsylvania.org/employee-ownership-businesses/)**
- Provides education and connections for businesses considering employee ownership
- Helps owners understand differences between ESOPs and cooperatives
- Connects businesses with qualified professionals

**[Workers to Owners Collaborative](https://becomingemployeeowned.org/workers-to-owners/)**
- National network supporting business conversions
- Coordinated by Democracy at Work Institute
- Provides tools, practices, and shared learning
- Goal: Expand volume, impacts, and accessibility of conversions nationwide

### Comparison: Worker Cooperative vs. ESOP

| Factor | Worker Cooperative | ESOP |
|--------|-------------------|------|
| Minimum Size | No minimum | Generally 15-20+ employees |
| Tax Benefits to Seller | Limited | Significant (1042 rollover) |
| Democratic Control | Direct (one member, one vote) | Varies by plan design |
| Ongoing Admin Costs | Lower | Higher (annual valuation, trustee fees) |
| Employee Cost | Member buy-in | Shares at no cost to employees |
| Best Fit | Smaller businesses, strong democratic culture | Larger businesses, tax-motivated sellers |

### Steps for Conversion

1. **Feasibility Assessment:** Evaluate whether business is suitable for worker ownership; analyze worker interest
2. **Valuation:** Obtain independent business valuation; negotiate fair price
3. **Structure Selection:** Choose between cooperative and ESOP (or hybrid)
4. **Financing Plan:** Develop capital structure (seller financing, cooperative loans, member equity)
5. **Legal Documentation:** Draft articles, bylaws/operating agreement, and purchase agreement
6. **Employee Education:** Train workers on cooperative governance and ownership
7. **Transition Period:** Implement gradual management transition

### Pennsylvania Success Example

**Advanced Conversion Technology** (Middletown, PA) - Employee-owned since 2021, demonstrating successful transition to employee ownership in Pennsylvania.

## 7. Common Pitfalls in Pennsylvania

### Legal and Structural Issues

**Minimum Member Requirement**
- Chapter 77 workers' cooperative corporations require five or more members
- Smaller groups should consider the LLC structure instead
- Plan for membership growth if starting near the minimum

**Naming Compliance**
- Cooperative corporations must include "cooperative" or an abbreviation in their name
- Failing to include this language will result in filing rejection
- LLCs structured as cooperatives do not have this requirement

**Complex Statutory Framework**
- Must navigate multiple chapters of Title 15 (Chapters 1, 5, 71, and 77)
- Consider consulting with an attorney familiar with Pennsylvania cooperative law
- The [Digital Media Law Project guide](https://www.dmlp.org/legal-guide/forming-journalism-cooperative-pennsylvania) notes the law's complexity

**Inadequate Operating Agreement Protections (for LLCs)**
- Future members could potentially remove cooperative provisions
- Solution: Work with experienced attorney to draft protective provisions
- Ensure amendment procedures require supermajority or unanimous consent for core cooperative principles

### Financial Challenges

**Undercapitalization**
- Insufficient startup capital is a leading cause of cooperative failure
- Lack of cash cushion to ride out market fluctuations or management mistakes
- Difficulty purchasing modern equipment
- Build adequate reserves and explore CDFI financing early

**Banking Difficulties**
- Traditional banks often expect a 5%+ individual owner for loan eligibility
- Banks and institutions unfamiliar with cooperative model require explanation
- Work with CDFIs and cooperative lenders familiar with the structure
- [PACA resources](https://philadelphia.coop/iyc/resources/) can help identify cooperative-friendly financial institutions

### Governance Mistakes

**Conflating Ownership and Management**
- Not every decision requires full member vote
- Cooperatives need managers who support cooperative principles and work within democratic structures
- Finding or re-educating managers from traditional organizations is challenging
- Establish clear decision-making levels in governance documents

**"Going Rogue" Problem**
- One member taking unilateral action because cooperatives move slower than sole-owner businesses
- Build patience for democratic process into organizational culture
- Establish clear accountability mechanisms

**Inadequate Operating Agreement/Bylaws**
- Weak or incomplete agreements lead to disputes
- Address member separation scenarios before they arise
- Include clear dispute resolution mechanisms
- Update documents as cooperative evolves

**Skipping Probationary Period**
- Granting full membership too quickly
- Establish reasonable probationary periods (typically 6-12 months)

### Operational Challenges

**Neglecting Business Fundamentals**
- Cooperative structure does not substitute for viable business model
- Product/service quality and customer focus remain essential
- Excessive costs, inadequate marketing, and lack of attention to customers are bad for any business

**Insufficient Member Education**
- Worker-owners need training in governance, finance, and cooperative principles
- Budget for ongoing education and development
- Utilize PACA, USFWC, and other resources for training programs

### Compliance Pitfalls

**Missing New Annual Report Requirements**
- Pennsylvania now requires annual reports (as of January 1, 2025)
- Calendar the appropriate deadline (June 30 for corporations, September 30 for LLCs)
- Budget for $7 filing fee

**Failing to Register for State Taxes**
- Operating without proper tax registrations
- Complete Pennsylvania Online Business Tax Registration through myPATH before commencing business

## 8. Frequently Asked Questions

### Q1: How many people do I need to start a worker cooperative in Pennsylvania?

For a Workers' Cooperative Corporation under Chapter 77, you need at least five members who are "farmers, mechanics, laborers or other persons." If you have fewer than five people, consider forming a Cooperative LLC instead, which has no minimum member requirement. You can later convert to a cooperative corporation as membership grows.

### Q2: What is the difference between a cooperative corporation and a cooperative LLC?

A cooperative corporation is explicitly recognized under Pennsylvania's Chapter 77 statute with specific requirements (five members minimum, "cooperative" in name, one member one vote mandated by law). A cooperative LLC is a standard LLC structured as a cooperative through its operating agreement, offering more flexibility but without dedicated statutory recognition. Both can achieve democratic worker ownership.

### Q3: How much does it cost to form a worker cooperative in Pennsylvania?

Basic formation costs include:
- Articles of Incorporation/Certificate of Organization: $125
- EIN: Free
- State tax registration: Free
- Philadelphia Commercial Activity License (if applicable): Free
- Annual Report (starting 2025): $7/year
- Optional name reservation: $70

Additional costs may include legal assistance, operating agreement drafting, publication fees, and any industry-specific licenses. Budget approximately $500-2,000 for basic formation without legal assistance, or $2,000-5,000 with attorney support.

### Q4: Do I need an attorney to form a worker cooperative?

While not legally required, consulting an attorney familiar with Pennsylvania cooperative law is strongly recommended. The [Digital Media Law Project](https://www.dmlp.org/legal-guide/forming-journalism-cooperative-pennsylvania) notes that "because the cooperative law is complex, you may wish to seek the assistance of an attorney." PACA and PaCEO can provide referrals to cooperative-experienced attorneys.

### Q5: How are worker cooperative members taxed in Pennsylvania?

Taxation depends on your legal structure:
- **Cooperative Corporations:** Generally taxed at the corporate level, with patronage dividends potentially deductible by the cooperative and taxable to members
- **Cooperative LLCs:** Default pass-through taxation; profits flow to members' individual returns
- **Pennsylvania State Tax:** Pennsylvania personal income tax applies at a flat 3.07% rate
- **Subchapter T:** Federal cooperative tax treatment may apply for patronage dividends

Consult a tax professional familiar with cooperative taxation for your specific situation.

### Q6: Can I convert my existing business to a worker cooperative?

Yes. Pennsylvania has strong support for business conversions through organizations like the [Pennsylvania Center for Employee Ownership](https://ownershippennsylvania.org/) and [Democracy at Work Institute](https://institute.coop/). Key steps include feasibility assessment, business valuation, structure selection, financing arrangement, and employee education. Conversions are particularly popular as succession planning for retiring business owners.

### Q7: What ongoing compliance requirements apply to Pennsylvania worker cooperatives?

- **Annual Report:** Due annually (June 30 for corporations, September 30 for LLCs); $7 fee
- **Tax Filings:** Federal and state tax returns based on entity type
- **Employer Obligations:** Withholding (3.07%), unemployment compensation, workers' compensation
- **Local Licenses:** Renew any required local business licenses
- **Corporate Records:** Maintain minutes of meetings and updated member records

### Q8: Where can I find support and training for my worker cooperative in Pennsylvania?

Key resources include:
- **[Philadelphia Area Cooperative Alliance (PACA)](https://philadelphia.coop/):** Regional training, Study Circles program, and technical assistance
- **[Pennsylvania Center for Employee Ownership](https://ownershippennsylvania.org/):** Education and professional connections
- **[U.S. Federation of Worker Cooperatives](https://www.usworker.coop/clinic/):** National technical assistance through Co-op Clinic
- **[Pennsylvania SBDC](https://www.pasbdc.org/):** Free business consulting at 15 locations statewide
- **[PA CDFI Network](https://pacdfinetwork.org/):** Financing and technical assistance from mission-driven lenders

Ready to start your worker cooperative in Pennsylvania? Here is your action plan:

1. **Assess Your Readiness**
   - Confirm you have at least 5 committed members (for cooperative corporation) or choose LLC structure

   - Review [Title 15, Chapter 77](https://www.legis.state.pa.us/WU01/LI/LI/CT/HTM/15/00.071..HTM) for cooperative corporation requirements
   - Search for your business name availability at [PA Department of State](https://www.pa.gov/agencies/dos/programs/business)
   - Consider reserving your name ($70) if you need time to prepare

### Formation Phase (Week 3-8)

4. **Secure Professional Support**
   - Consult with an attorney experienced in Pennsylvania cooperative law
   - Engage an accountant familiar with cooperative taxation
   - Connect with cooperative-friendly lenders if financing is needed

5. **Draft Governance Documents**
   - Develop comprehensive bylaws or operating agreement using [DAWI templates](https://institute.coop/bylaw-and-operating-agreement-templates)
   - Create membership agreement template
   - Establish financial and personnel policies

6. **File Formation Documents**
   - File Articles of Incorporation or Certificate of Organization ($125)
   - Publish required newspaper notices
   - Obtain EIN from IRS
   - Register with [Pennsylvania Department of Revenue](https://www.pa.gov/services/revenue/register-my-business-for-taxes) via myPATH

### Launch Phase (Week 9+)

7. **Complete Operational Setup**
   - Open business bank account
   - Obtain necessary local licenses and permits (including Philadelphia CAL if applicable)
   - Set up payroll and accounting systems
   - Purchase required insurance (including workers' compensation)

8. **Invest in Member Development**
   - Conduct cooperative governance training for all members
   - Establish regular meeting schedules
   - Build your cooperative culture intentionally
   - Consider [USFWC membership](https://www.usworker.coop/) for ongoing support
